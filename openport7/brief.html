Executive Summary

The GPA wants to convene actors and experts engaged in the process of opening ports and evaluating the needs and viability of closed ports. Acknowledging a critical moment for port authorities worldwide at a new global juncture (see GPA-declaration ‘GPA 2016: ‘the culture comes from the ports’ – re-phrasing the IOPS!’), the GPA will be consulting on a set of revised frameworks and certification standards for evaluating the continued use(-value) of ports globally for open forms of human interaction, extending to interspecies and interplanetary endeavours. GPA will also provide its own assets and expertise, channeling a long tradition of critical port governance on a global scale to the Berlin encounter, the first in the new phase of GPA´s 7th “evolution campaign” around the cultural structure of global ports.
To achieve an exchange that all existing port authorities are benefiting from in mutual exchange and consultation, the GPA has set up a temporary open.port in Berlin at Schillerpalais, close to the port of Tempelhof, the heritage airport site and current ground for different, even conflicting aspirations to re-port.
Alongside the open.port effort the GPA is collaborating with Transmediale to exchange expertise on new techniques and skillful technologies to open up and develop post-digital spaces with the on the ground interests of strategical Berlin port.
